# Democratic Amendment Process & Governance

## How Values Can Change: Constitutional Amendments
The constitution isn't static. It evolves through democratic amendments.

### Who Can Propose Amendments?
**Amendment Rights**:
- ✅ Any BacklinkRadio team member
- ✅ Artist council representatives (appointed by artists)
- ✅ Community representatives (elected by listeners, optional feature)
- ❌ Individual listeners cannot propose (feedback via council)
- ❌ Sponsors cannot propose

### Amendment Proposal Process

#### Step 1: Proposal Submission
```text
Proposer creates amendment:
├─ Current rule (what are we changing?)
├─ Proposed rule (what will it become?)
├─ Rationale (why this change?)
├─ Impact analysis (who does this affect?)
├─ Implementation plan (how to deploy?)
└─ Rollback plan (what if it fails?)

Submitted to governance system
```

#### Step 2: 14-Day Feedback Period
```text
Amendment published for feedback:
├─ Team members comment
├─ Artist council feedback collected
├─ Impact analysis validated
├─ Engineers assess feasibility
└─ Questions answered

14 days for feedback/discussion
```

#### Step 3: Team Voting (Supermajority: 66% required)
```text
All team members vote:
- YES: Support amendment
- NO: Oppose amendment
- ABSTAIN: No position

Supermajority (66%) required to pass:
├─ 10 team members
├─ 7 votes YES needed (66%)
├─ Passes to artist council
└─ If not 66%: Amendment rejected (STOP)
```

#### Step 4: Artist Council Voting (Simple Majority: 50%+1 required)
```text
Artist council votes:
- YES: Approve amendment
- NO: Veto amendment
- ABSTAIN: No position

Simple majority (50%+1) required:
├─ 5 council members
├─ 3 votes YES needed
├─ Amendment approved for implementation
└─ If not majority: Amendment rejected (STOP)
```
